# London's Unemployment Pulse

## Overview

**London's Unemployment Pulse** is a Tableau data visualization project that explores unemployment trends in London between **2001 and 2025**. The visualization combines long-term trend analysis with demographic breakdowns to reveal not only how unemployment has changed over time, but also which age groups have been most affected.

The project presents unemployment through two complementary perspectives:

* **Unemployment Rate (%)** – measures the intensity of unemployment.
* **Estimated Number of People Unemployed** – measures the scale of unemployment.

Together, these views highlight how percentage-based trends and absolute counts can tell very different stories.

---

## Key Questions

* How has unemployment in London evolved over the last two decades?
* Which age groups consistently experience higher unemployment rates?
* Do age groups with the highest unemployment rates also represent the largest number of unemployed people?
* How do economic shocks impact different segments of the workforce?

---

## Dashboard Features

### Trend Analysis

* Long-term unemployment trends from 2001–2025
* Gradient line charts to emphasize periods of rising and falling unemployment
* Interactive tooltips for detailed exploration

### Age-Based Analysis

* Small multiple (trellis) bar charts comparing unemployment across age groups
* Time-period segmentation to reveal changing patterns over time
* Easy comparison between younger and older workforce cohorts

### Interactive Elements

* Parameter-driven views
* Dynamic filtering
* Responsive tooltips and annotations

---

## Key Insights

* Younger workers (16–17 and 18–24) consistently experience the highest unemployment rates.
* High unemployment rates do not always correspond to the highest number of unemployed individuals.
* Prime working-age groups may account for a larger share of unemployed people despite lower unemployment rates.
* Labour market disruptions affect age groups differently, highlighting structural inequalities within the workforce.
